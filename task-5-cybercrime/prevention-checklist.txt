**Stay Safe Online – A Checklist for College Students in India**

1. **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP or banking details** with anyone. If someone says they are from the bank or a payment app do not give them this information. Banks will never ask for it. They will not call you to ask for your UPI PIN, OTP or banking details. Remember, UPI PIN, OTP or banking details are secret.

2. **Be careful with QR codes and payment requests**. Do not scan QR codes from people you do not know. When someone sends you a payment request make sure you are sending money to the person. When you enter your PIN in UPI it means you are sending money not getting it. So think before entering your PIN. Always check QR codes and payment requests carefully.

3. **Download apps from sources**. You should use Google Play Store or Apple App Store to download apps. Do not download apps from sources. This will help keep your phone and UPI PIN, OTP or banking details safe.

4. **Add a layer of security** to your accounts. Enable two-factor authentication on your email, media and banking apps. This helps keep college students in India accounts safe from hackers. Two-factor authentication is like a lock on your accounts.

5. **Avoid using Wi-Fi for transactions**. When you use Wi-Fi for banking or UPI payments hackers can intercept your data. This is not safe for college students in India. Try to use your data for transactions instead of Wi-Fi.

6. **Check website addresses carefully**. Some fake websites may look like ones but have spelling mistakes or strange links. Make sure the website is genuine before logging in or making payments. Check the website address before entering your UPI PIN, OTP or banking details.

7. **Be cautious when sharing details online**. Do not share your phone number, address or ID proofs on media or unknown websites. College students in India should be careful when sharing details online. Remember, your personal details are private.

8. **Update your passwords regularly**. Use passwords with letters, numbers and symbols. Do not use the password for all accounts. This will help keep your accounts safe from hackers. Update your passwords every months.

9. **Verify buyers and sellers online**. Before making transactions on platforms like OLX, Instagram or WhatsApp Marketplace ensure you are dealing with people. Do not send money to strangers. Verify the buyer or seller before making a transaction.

10. **Report cybercrime**. If you are a victim or notice any activity report it through the National Cyber Crime Portal: **cybercrime.gov.in** or the Cyber Crime Helpline: **1930**. This will help keep college students in India safe from cybercrime.

By following this checklist college students in India can stay online protect their money and avoid becoming victims of cybercrime. College students, in India should follow these tips to stay online and protect their UPI PIN, OTP or banking details.
